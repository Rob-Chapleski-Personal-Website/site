Meddling in the Middle-Tech

My hobbies gravitate together the highs and lows of technology. In this madly and rapidly advancing age of the cutting-edge and next big technological breakthrough, I love finding new comfortable places to toy with what are seen as today's basic and fundamental elements, which themselves served as the technological forefront of the recent past.

Amateur Radio

Yes, THAT ham. Amateur Extra, call sign: KY4ZO.
Light waves are freaking cool. Ripples of energy flinging through space letting us do all kinds of crazy things. The more we understand about them, the more we can do with them. Me? I particularly like APRS, the sending of digital packets over radio waves. Think GPS positioning reports, or text messaging. Just like your phone, but with technical control over every part of the process.
If I've operated my APRS station recently, you can see what I've been up to here!

Single Board Computing and Retro Gaming

I like to tinker with the magic I grew up around. (Don't tell me you weren't fascinated by cash registers as a child!) My idea of a good side-project (and ebay haul) has me starting with an thermal receipt printer and a RaspberryPi and ending up with a wifi-connected, AirPlay enabled greyscale thermal shopping-list and photo printer.
Speaking of magic I grew up around-- my taste in video games remains the same as it always has been-- 2D, blocky, platforming, puzzlers all the way. Mario, Sonic, Tetris. My favorite game of all time? Link's Awakening for Game Boy.
